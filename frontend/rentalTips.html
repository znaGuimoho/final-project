10 Essential Rental Tips

Whether you're a first-time renter or a seasoned landlord, these proven tips will save you time, money, and headaches.

Smart Rental Tips Everyone Should Know

01

Read the Lease Twice

Never sign anything you don’t fully understand. Pay special attention to rules about pets, guests, maintenance, and early termination fees.

02

Document Everything

Take dated photos/videos of the property on move-in day. This protects your security deposit when you move out.

03

Get Renter’s Insurance

It’s usually only $10–20/month and covers your belongings in case of theft, fire, or water damage — landlord insurance doesn’t cover your stuff!

04

Know Your Rights

Research local tenant laws. Many cities require 24–48 hour notice before a landlord can enter, limit rent increases, and more.

05

Report Issues in Writing

Always email or text maintenance requests. This creates a paper trail if bigger problems arise later.

06

Budget the Real Cost

Rent shouldn’t exceed 30% of your income. Don’t forget utilities, parking, laundry, internet, and renter’s insurance.

07

Meet the Neighbors

A quick hello can tell you a lot about noise levels, community vibe, and whether the building is well-managed.

08

Ask About Renewal Early

Start the conversation 90 days before your lease ends. Good tenants often get better renewal offers if they ask early.

09

Understand Security Deposits

Most states require landlords to return your deposit (minus legitimate deductions) within 14–60 days after move-out.

10

Leave on Good Terms

Clean thoroughly and give proper notice. A positive reference from a previous landlord is gold for your next application.
